About

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Duke University, advised by Prof. Neil Gong. My research studies the security and safety of generative AI — spanning LLM agents and prompt injection, watermarking and detection of AI-generated content, safety of (multimodal) large language models, and trustworthy machine learning.

I am currently a Research Scientist Intern at Meta Superintelligence Labs, where I study reward hacking in RL-trained coding agents. Previously, I interned at TikTok Privacy Innovation Labs. I received my B.S. in Data Science from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC).

I am actively looking for full-time research scientist / applied scientist opportunities in AI security and trustworthy ML — please feel free to reach out.
